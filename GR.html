Game Recommendations 

Paper's, Please

Papers, Please puts you in the shoes of an immigration officer in a dystopian, Eastern Bloc-style country. Your job is to check documents and decide who gets to cross the border, but it’s more intense than it sounds. The game throws moral dilemmas at you, forcing you to choose between following the rules or helping people in need.

You’ll juggle changing regulations while trying to keep your family safe. It’s a game that makes you think about ethics and consequences, all wrapped up in a tense, pixel-art world that keeps you hooked.

Kingdom Come Deliverance

Kingdom Come: Deliverance is like a deep dive into the medieval ages, but way more intense than most games. You play as Henry, a blacksmith’s son who gets pulled into a brutal civil war after his village is burned down. You’re not some super-powerful hero here—you start off barely able to fight, read, or survive on your own, and every skill you pick up has to be earned.

The game’s super detailed, with historical armor, weapons, and even herbs you can collect. Combat’s tough, and you can’t just run in swinging; you need timing and patience, or you’re done.


Steam Page






Max Payne

Max Payne is gritty, dark, and absolutely badass. You play as Max, a cop turned vigilante out for revenge after his family is brutally murdered. The story is intense, with a graphic novel style that gives it a cool, noir vibe, and Max’s monologues are just dripping with angst and cynicism.

The gameplay is where it really shines, though—this was one of the first games to nail slow-motion “bullet time” gunfights, and it’s still fun as hell. Diving through the air in slow motion while blasting bad guys just never gets old. It’s a bit rough around the edges by today’s standards, but if you’re down for a dark story with some classic action, Max Payne still hits hard.

(Just a heads up, when playing this on PC, please make sure you get the fixes, as without them the game will be unplayable due to its age)

Hypnospace Outlaw

HypnoSpace Outlaw is like stepping back into the weird internet of the ‘90s, and it nails the vibe perfectly. You play as an “Enforcer,” patrolling a dreamlike online space to track down copyright violations, hackers, and all kinds of weird internet stuff. It’s packed with bizarre websites, cringy graphics, and over-the-top music, and exploring it all feels like a nostalgia trip, even if you didn’t actually live through it.

The writing is hilarious and full of personality, and you’ll probably end up way more invested in this digital world than you’d expect. It also has a banger soundtrack with so many artists I can't even count.

Postal 2

Postal 2 is one of the most insane games I’ve ever played. You’re the Postal Dude, just trying to get through a regular week, but everything goes off the rails almost immediately. You can handle situations however you want, whether that’s using a shovel on people, lighting stuff on fire, or even pissing on them. You can even play the entire game passively, its as the box cover said "It's only as violent as you are". It’s completely crude and honestly offensive, but that’s what makes it fun if you’re into dark humor.

The controls are kind of clunky, but it weirdly adds to the charm. You’re definitely not here for some deep story or polished gameplay—it’s all about laughing at just how ridiculous and messed up everything is.

Pretty much sums the game up

Batman: Arkham Asylum

This game really makes you FEEL like Batman. Batman: Arkham Asylum transports you into the heart of a twisted, isolated psychiatric facility rather than the streets of Gotham, and it’s an experience you won’t forget. You step into the Dark Knight’s shoes as he battles the Joker and other notorious villains inside Arkham's eerie, claustrophobic walls.

The fluid combat system lets you take down enemies with precision, while the stealth mechanics let you pick off foes from the shadows like a true predator. With a gripping story, fantastic voice acting (including Mark Hamill's iconic Joker), and a dark, atmospheric setting, Arkham Asylum offers a thrilling adventure that captures the essence of being Batman.

Deus Ex

Deus Ex is a classic in the immersive sim genre, mixing a deep story with gameplay that gives you a lot of freedom, though it’s starting to feel a bit outdated. Set in a dystopian future, you play as JC Denton, a cybernetically enhanced agent trying to uncover a global conspiracy. What makes the game stand out is the way it lets you approach each mission in different ways—whether that’s through stealth, hacking, or combat.

The choices you make actually impact the story, which is a big part of the immersive sim experience. While the graphics and some gameplay elements show their age, the story, world-building, and the weight of your decisions still feel relevant. Even though it’s a bit old now, Deus Ex is still a must-play for anyone who likes cyberpunk, decision-driven gameplay, or immersive sims.

Battlefield 1

Ok, so I realized I haven't put a multiplayer shooter here, so here we go. Battlefield 1 is a fantastic game that pulls you into the chaos of World War I, with massive, dynamic battles that make you feel like you're right in the middle of the action. The game’s large-scale maps, complete with tanks, planes, and even cavalry, create an immersive experience where every match feels like an epic struggle.

While it isn’t the most historically accurate representation of WWI, it does a great job of capturing the intensity and scale of the war, even if it takes some creative liberties with weapons and combat. The teamwork and strategy are essential to success, and the visuals and sound design really bring the time period to life, making you feel like you're truly fighting in the trenches. It also has one of the greatest opening to a game I have ever seen, just go play it damn it.
